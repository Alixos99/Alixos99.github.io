NEW YORK CITY

Established as a Dutch trading post in 1624, New York City, or the "Big Apple," has grown into a dynamic metropolis known for its iconic skyline and cultural diversity. From its colonial roots to becoming a beacon of opportunity, the city has witnessed waves of immigration, economic booms, and cultural movements, shaping its identity as a global hub of finance, arts, and innovation. Today, the city's historic landmarks, such as the Statue of Liberty and Times Square, stand as symbols of its enduring significance and resilience through the changing tides of time.
